The Chords Technique

The chord is a variation of the detache in which multiple notes is being played. String crossing using the bow is more likely the method of playing multiple notes here. Chords can be very hard to play.


Chords is also known as double stops, which plays two notes at the same time and also triple stops, which plays three notes at the same time. They are usually seed as stacked notes in sheet music.

It can be played three ways: (1) Broken chords in which lower notes are played first. (2) Unbroken chords in which simultaneously all notes are played blending the lower and higher notes. (3) Turned chords wherein all notes are equally sustained, mostly used in polyphonic music.

The most common example of chords is J.S. Bach's Chaconne and Johannes Brahms Violin Concerto.
