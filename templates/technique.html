Bowing Tutorial

Detache

Detaché is a playing technique on violin and other string instruments that calls for broad but separate bow strokes. In printed sheet music, the notes simply are not slurred. Sonically, detaché achieves a median balance between the fluid legato technique and jaunty staccato technique.

Chords

A chord is a set of three or more different notes. These notes must play of the same time and be in a specific key. Since playing three strings at once on the violin is so difficult, a dyad or two notes of the chord, is what is played.

Son File

A variant of Detache bowign technique that is characterized by Beginning at the tip and keeping the bow parallel to the end of the fingerboard and the bridge, steadily move the bow to the frog, generating a pleasant sound while counting.

Legato

Legato is an Italian word that means “tied together.” In stringed-instrument lingo, it signifies smooth and connected bow strokes, which can be connected by slurs or by smooth bow changes. Artistically speaking, legato holds the key to bringing out the best tone and most melodious tendencies of the instrument

Portato

Portato is a bowing technique for stringed instruments, in which successive notes are gently re-articulated while being joined under a single continuing bow stroke. It achieves a kind of pulsation or undulation, rather than separating the notes.
